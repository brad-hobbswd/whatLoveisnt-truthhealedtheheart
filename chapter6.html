Chapter Six

Opening Up

Healing often begins with safety and grows stronger through trust.

The weeks after the youth group project passed quietly, but something inside Justin had begun to shift. He no longer flinched when someone touched his arm. He no longer spent nights replaying Sam’s words or actions. The panic still surfaced occasionally, but it was smaller, quieter, easier to manage. He could feel it fading.

Brad was always nearby, steady and patient. He did not push Justin to talk about the past, and he did not demand explanations or confessions. He simply offered a hand on the shoulder, a shared smile, a quiet joke. Slowly, Justin realized that trust could grow again, even after betrayal.

One evening, they sat on a park bench after a long walk. The city lights reflected in the river, soft and golden. Justin had been unusually quiet. Brad noticed but did not prod. He handed Justin a bottle of water, letting the silence linger like an invitation rather than a burden.

“I don’t usually do this,” Justin said finally, his voice low. “Talk about myself. I… I guess I’m afraid of what comes out when I do.”

Brad nodded, his expression gentle.

“You can take your time. You don’t have to tell me everything at once. Just whatever feels safe.”

Justin swallowed hard. He wanted to tell someone that he had been hurt in ways no one should ever be hurt. That Sam had left him broken and terrified. But he also wanted to protect himself from the shame he felt.

“I guess I just never knew what real care looked like,” Justin admitted. “I thought love was supposed to hurt. That’s what I grew up thinking, and that’s what Sam… what he made me think.”

Brad stayed quiet, just listening. No judgment. No rush. Just listening.

“I’m learning,” Justin continued, “that love doesn’t have to feel like fear. That people can care and not hurt you. That boundaries aren’t walls, they’re protection.”

He looked at Brad, eyes searching. “I don’t know if I’ll ever fully believe it, but I want to try.”

Brad smiled softly. “That’s all I ask. Just try. And I’ll be here while you do.”

Justin felt something warm bloom inside, a feeling he had not allowed himself to feel in years. Safety. Trust. Acceptance. Not perfection, not promises of forever, just the quiet reassurance that someone would stay beside him while he learned to walk again.

It was not romance, at least not yet. It was something deeper, something more important: a foundation. Justin realized he could rebuild himself without Sam’s shadow hanging over him. He could trust, slowly and cautiously, and still survive if the past tried to catch him.

For the first time, Justin allowed himself to imagine a life where he could laugh without guilt, sleep without fear, and wake without dread. Brad was not a fix. He was a witness to his recovery, a mirror showing Justin the strength he had not believed he possessed.

As the evening turned to night, Justin leaned back on the bench, feeling the weight of the world lift just enough to breathe. The city hummed quietly around them, steady and unrelenting. Justin finally understood that healing was not a destination. It was a series of small victories, each one building on the last, each one a step toward freedom.

He glanced at Brad, still sitting patiently beside him, and whispered softly:

“Thank you for staying.”

Brad smiled. “Always.”

For the first time, Justin felt safe letting the past remain in the past while he stepped into a future he could finally call his own.
