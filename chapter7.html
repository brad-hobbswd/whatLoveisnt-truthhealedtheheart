Chapter Seven

Standing Tall

Healing becomes visible when peace no longer feels temporary and strength begins to feel natural.

Spring had arrived, and with it came a sense of quiet renewal. Justin could feel it in the air, in the warm sunlight, in the soft breeze, in the way the city seemed to hum with life. It mirrored the change inside him. He no longer flinched at loud noises, no longer woke drenched in sweat from nightmares. Each day felt lighter, freer.

He was sitting at a café one morning when his phone buzzed. His heart skipped. He recognized the number immediately.

Justin. It’s Sam. We need to talk.

For a moment, panic clawed at him, just as it had in the past. Then he looked around, breathed deeply, and remembered everything he had built, the boundaries, the trust in himself, the safety he had created with Brad. He did not answer.

Another message came, insistent and desperate.

I made a mistake. Please. I need you.

Justin stared at the screen and then locked the phone. He did not feel fear this time. He felt calm. He knew Sam had not truly changed, but he also knew he was stronger than the hold Sam once had over him. Survival was not enough. Healing meant choosing himself.

Later that day, he met Brad at the park. They walked along the river, sunlight dancing on the water. Justin’s steps were confident, measured, no longer weighed down by dread.

“Something is wrong?” Brad asked, noticing Justin’s tight smile.

“Sam,” Justin said simply. “He is reaching out again.”

Brad nodded. “And?”

“I am not responding,” Justin said, firm. “I have built my life. I have built myself. I will not let him take that away.”

Brad smiled, pride clear in his eyes.

“Good. That is exactly the choice you need to make. You are not his anymore.”

Justin felt something he had not felt in years, control over his own life. He realized he could choose who entered his world, who stayed, and who deserved his energy. It was not about hatred or revenge. It was about self preservation, strength, and dignity.

As they walked, Brad reached over and gave Justin’s shoulder a light squeeze.

“You have come so far. I am proud of you. And I will be here every step of the way.”

Justin smiled genuinely, a laugh tugging at the corner of his lips.

“Thanks, Brad. I think I am finally starting to believe that it is possible to be safe, to trust, and maybe even to love again someday.”

Brad’s eyes softened.

“Whenever you are ready, Justin. No rush. No pressure. Just the world waiting for you to step fully into it.”

For the first time, Justin felt the truth of those words. His past with Sam no longer defined him. The abuse, the fear, the sleepless nights were memories, painful but no longer controlling. He had reclaimed his life, piece by piece, and built a foundation strong enough to stand on.

They reached the edge of the river, watching the sunlight ripple across the water. Justin took a deep breath, filling his lungs with freedom.

“I am done surviving,” he said softly. “I am ready to live.”

Brad smiled, and in that moment Justin knew he was not alone. He had built a life with people who cared for him, who respected his boundaries, and who would never allow him to fall back into fear.

The past was behind him. The future was his. For the first time in years, Justin felt the peace he had been searching for, steady, unwavering, and real.
