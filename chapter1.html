Chapter One

The beginning of fear often arrives quietly before anyone realizes survival has replaced peace.

Justin learned very early how to read the atmosphere of a room before anyone spoke.

Tension had a sound. Silence carried weight. Footsteps down the hallway could determine whether the evening would remain calm or suddenly collapse into fear.

Nobody outside the home noticed the exhaustion living behind his smile. Teachers called him respectful. Friends described him as quiet and kind. Church members praised his behavior.

Inside, however, Justin constantly monitored emotions, reactions, moods, and tones in order to avoid conflict. Every conversation felt dangerous. Every mistake felt catastrophic.

Over time, survival became instinct.

Apologies escaped his mouth even when he had done nothing wrong. Fear trained him to believe peace could only exist when everybody else remained satisfied.

Nobody taught him what healthy love looked like.

Control disguised itself as protection. Manipulation disguised itself as concern. Emotional instability disguised itself as passion.

Justin slowly began believing that anxiety was normal. Walking on eggshells felt familiar. Silence became safer than honesty.

Deep inside, however, another part of him quietly questioned whether love was truly supposed to hurt this much.

That question frightened him.

Questions have a way of awakening people who spent years surviving environments they were taught never to challenge.

For the first time in a very long time, Justin began wondering whether fear and love were never meant to coexist.
