Foreword

Some stories are written for entertainment. Others are written because silence became too painful to carry.

What Love Isn’t was born from the reality that many people grow up believing pain, fear, manipulation, silence, emotional control, and instability are normal parts of love.

Many hearts survive relationships, families, friendships, churches, and environments that slowly convince them they deserve mistreatment. Over time, survival replaces peace. Fear replaces trust. Silence replaces honesty.

This story exists to confront those lies.

Real love does not destroy identity. Genuine care does not manipulate emotions. Healthy relationships do not require people to abandon boundaries in order to keep peace.

Healing often begins when someone finally realizes that what harmed them was never love in the first place.

Through Justin’s journey, this story explores trauma, emotional survival, fear, healing, boundaries, trust, and the slow process of learning how to feel safe again.

For every reader carrying invisible wounds, may these pages remind you that restoration remains possible.

Peace still exists.

Safe love still exists.

Your story is not over.
