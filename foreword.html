Foreword

Some stories are not just meant to be read. They are meant to be felt.

Some stories are not just meant to be read; they are meant to be felt. They speak to the parts of us that have been hidden, silenced, or broken. This is one of those stories.

What Love Isn’t: The Truth That Healed the Heart is more than a tale of heartbreak and healing; it is a testament to the resilience of the human spirit. It captures the journey of someone who has endured pain and betrayal, who has faced fear and trauma, and yet finds the courage to reclaim life, trust, and love. Justin’s journey is a mirror for anyone who has struggled with the shadows of abuse, the lingering echoes of toxic relationships, or the paralyzing grip of fear.

This story also reminds us that healing is rarely a straight path. It is messy, slow, and often painful. But it is possible. It shows that love; real love, is not about control, manipulation, or destruction. It is about safety, trust, patience, and care. It is about the people who choose to stay when the world pushes you away, who believe in you even when you cannot believe in yourself.

As you read this book, I hope you see yourself on Justin’s journey. I hope you feel the tension, the fear, the relief, and the triumph. And most of all, I hope it gives you hope; that no matter what you have endured, there is a path to freedom, to peace, and to love that does not harm.

This is a story of survival, courage, and the transformative power of trust. May it inspire anyone who has struggled to find their way back to the light.
