Preface

Some beginnings are born from endings.

Every story has a beginning, but some beginnings are born from endings; the endings of what no longer serves us, the endings of relationships that hurt more than they heal, the endings that force us to face ourselves. What Love Isn’t: The Truth That Healed the Heart is one of those stories.

This book is a journey through pain, fear, and the aftermath of abuse. It is about a young man named Justin who endured betrayal and harm yet found the courage to reclaim his life. It is about boundaries, healing, and discovering that love does not have to hurt to be real. It is about learning to trust again, to live fully, and to open one’s heart to connection that uplifts rather than diminishes.

The story is not just for survivors of abuse, though they may see themselves most clearly in Justin’s experiences. It is for anyone who has ever felt trapped by fear, weighed down by shame, or hesitant to believe that life can be safe and joyful again. It is for the friends, the supporters, the quiet heroes who show up and stay, helping others reclaim themselves.

In these pages, you will witness a journey that is raw, honest, and ultimately hopeful. You will see the complexity of human emotions, the longing for love, the struggle to trust, the tension between fear and courage, and you will see that healing is possible, even when the path is long and uncertain.

This is not a tale of perfection or instant resolution. It is a story of resilience, growth, and the quiet power of steadfast love, the kind of love that does not demand, does not harm, and does not falter.

As you read, you may find pieces of yourself in Justin’s journey. May it encourage you to set boundaries, to seek support, and to believe that you, too, can find freedom, peace, and love that heals.

This is the story of a person’s survival and the love that helped him rise. It is my hope that it becomes a guide, a comfort, and a reminder that no one is ever truly alone on the path to healing.
