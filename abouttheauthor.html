About the Author

Stories written from the belief that healing, peace, restoration, honesty, and safe love remain possible after painful seasons of life.

Bradley Hobbs is a writer, creative designer, and storyteller passionate about themes of emotional healing, restoration, boundaries, faith, identity, and discovering peace after trauma.

Through deeply reflective storytelling, Bradley explores the realities many people quietly carry within themselves including fear, emotional exhaustion, manipulation, rejection, shame, and the long journey toward healing.

What Love Isn’t was written to encourage readers who have survived emotionally painful relationships, unhealthy environments, and seasons where peace felt impossible to find.

His writing focuses on honesty, compassion, emotional truth, healing, and helping wounded hearts understand that genuine love never destroys dignity or demands self abandonment.

Beyond writing, Bradley also works through Brad Hobbs Web Design, creating visually immersive websites and digital experiences designed with creativity, atmosphere, and emotional storytelling in mind.

Through every project, one central message continues appearing repeatedly.

Healing remains possible.

Peace remains possible.

Safe love still exists.
