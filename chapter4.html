Chapter Four

Breakthroughs and Boundaries

Healing sometimes looks like choosing safety after years of surviving storms.

It had been nearly three months since Justin began attending church with Trisha and getting to know Brad. Life had grown lighter in quiet, almost unnoticeable ways. He could sleep a little longer. He could laugh without forcing it. He could even imagine a future that did not include Sam’s shadow. But the past has a way of finding you, no matter how far you run.

It happened on a rainy Wednesday afternoon. Justin was sitting on the couch, scrolling through his phone, when a message appeared from an unknown number.

Justin. It’s Sam. We need to talk.

His stomach dropped. His hands turned cold. Every muscle in his body tightened, just as they had during the worst nights with Sam. For a moment, he sat frozen, remembering every lie, every betrayal, every bruise. Then he thought of Brad. He thought of Trisha. He thought of the slow, steady kindness that had helped him start to breathe again.

He did not reply.

The phone buzzed again.

Please. I want to see you. Just one last time.

Justin’s mind told him to ignore it. But a small part of him, the part that still wanted to believe in the man he once loved, hesitated.

A knock sounded at the door. His heart raced. He was not expecting anyone. When he opened it, Brad stood there, rain dripping from his jacket.

“I had a feeling you might need backup,” Brad said gently, holding out a towel. “I know the look you get when you see Sam’s name on your phone.”

Justin exhaled, his voice unsteady. “He says he wants to see me. One last time. I don’t know what to do.”

Brad stepped closer but did not touch him.

“You don’t owe him anything,” he said. “Not a word, not a meeting, not a goodbye that puts you in harm’s way. You’re allowed to protect yourself.”

Tears welled in Justin’s eyes. He had never known what it felt like for someone to care without condition, to stand beside him when his world felt unsteady.

“I want to believe he’s changed,” Justin whispered.

Brad shook his head softly. “He hasn’t. And even if he had, that isn’t your responsibility. You’re not required to give him another chance. You’re responsible for keeping yourself safe.”

Justin nodded slowly. His hands trembled as he picked up the phone and typed a single reply.

I am not available. Do not contact me again.

He set the phone down and leaned back, shaking. Brad sat beside him in silence, giving him space to breathe, to feel the mix of fear and relief settling in his chest.

After a while, Justin let himself rest against Brad’s shoulder. It felt safe. He did not have to flinch or brace himself. The storm that had defined his life with Sam was finally over. Setting that boundary did not build a wall. It built a shield.

Healing, Justin realized, was not about erasing the past or pretending the pain never happened. It was about standing tall, scars and all, and deciding who and what could have access to his heart.

Brad placed a hand on his shoulder.

“You’re stronger than you think,” he said quietly.

Justin closed his eyes and nodded. “Maybe. But I couldn’t have done it alone.”

“You won’t have to,” Brad said.

And for the first time in months, Justin felt the weight of fear lift just enough to let him breathe. He was not free from the past, but he was free to move forward. That freedom, at last, felt like the beginning of something real.
