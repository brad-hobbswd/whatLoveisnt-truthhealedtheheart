Epilogue

Living Free

Healing becomes beautiful when peace finally begins feeling like home.

Months had passed since Justin and Brad had taken their first steps into life together. Justin had moved into a small apartment of his own, a place that felt truly his, where he could breathe and make choices without fear. The walls were bare at first, but slowly he filled them with color, paintings, and little reminders of the life he had reclaimed.

Brad was there almost every day, whether for breakfast on lazy mornings, evening walks, or quiet nights spent watching movies on the couch. Their life was simple, steady, and safe, everything Justin had once believed impossible.

One Saturday morning, they sat on the balcony, sunlight warming their faces. Justin sipped coffee while Brad strummed his guitar, humming a soft tune. The city below pulsed with energy, but up here, everything felt calm.

“Remember the first time we met at church?” Justin asked, smiling.

Brad laughed. “I do. You looked like you did not want to be anywhere near me.”

“I did not,” Justin admitted. “I was scared. Scared of trusting anyone again. Scared of Sam showing up, of everything falling apart. But you made it easy to breathe.”

Brad reached across the table and took Justin’s hand.

“I did not do anything magical. I just stayed. Sometimes that is all it takes, being there when someone needs to know it is safe to trust again.”

Justin nodded, feeling quiet gratitude swell inside him.

“I never thought I would get here. I never thought I could love again without fear.”

“You can,” Brad said softly. “And you can trust me. Always.”

Justin squeezed his hand.

“I do. More than I ever thought I could.”

They sat together, the morning stretching gently around them. Justin thought about the nights of fear, the bruises, the lies, and the darkness he had endured. He remembered the pain, but it no longer had power over him. He had survived, healed, and learned to set boundaries. He had built a life of safety, trust, and love.

Looking at Brad, he realized something else. Love did not have to hurt. It did not have to destroy. It could heal, nurture, and lift him higher than he had ever imagined.

For the first time, Justin felt fully free. He was free to love, free to live, and free to be himself.

Brad leaned closer and pressed a gentle kiss to his temple.

“Here is to us,” he whispered.

Justin smiled and closed his eyes, letting the peace settle deep into his bones. “Here is to us,” he echoed.

As the sunlight spilled across their balcony and the city came alive beneath them, Justin knew without a doubt that this was only the beginning. The past could not touch him. Fear could not reach him. Love, real, safe, healing love, would carry him forward.

Together, they had found freedom. Together, they had found home.
